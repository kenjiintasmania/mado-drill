EIKEN Grade Pre-2 Plus — Speaking (Second-Stage) Interview

Guide & Mock-Interview Script · English Dept. · June 2026
⚠️ A practical summary (June 2026) for running practice interviews. EIKEN does not publish per-question point values — confirm details on the official page and the Virtual Interview animation.

1 · Quick Overview

Format
1-on-1, single examiner · all English
Length
about 6–7 minutes
Level
between Pre-2 and Grade 2 (upper-secondary)
Materials
problem card: ~60-word passage + 3-panel illustration

2 · Format at a Glance

TaskWhat the student doesWhat it checks
Reading aloudRead the ~60-word passagePronunciation, intonation, fluency
No.1Answer a question about the passageComprehension; answer drawn from the text
No.2 (Narration)Describe a 3-panel illustration; 20 sec prep, must begin with the sentence on the cardPast / past-continuous, sequencing, actions & feelings
No.3Give own opinion on the card's topicOpinion + reason(s)
No.4Give own opinion on a general/social topicOpinion + reason(s)
The student turns the card face-down before No.3 & No.4.

3 · Assessment Criteria (5 viewpoints)

Content
Pronunciation
Vocabulary / Grammar
Amount of information
Attitude
Reported on the CSE scale (Speaking out of 625; passing ≈ 427 — confirm officially). Attitude = active willingness to communicate: eye contact, audible voice, not giving up. Item-level points are not published.

4 · Coaching Tips for ALTs

  • Push for full sentences and a reason ("…because…").
  • For No.2, encourage time markers ("One day," "Ten minutes later," "That afternoon,") and feelings ("She looked worried.").
  • Reward attitude: eye contact, a smile, a clear voice — and tell the student it counts.
  • Teach stalling ("Let me see…") and asking again ("Could you say that again, please?") so silence never wins.
  • Stay encouraging; give corrections after the run, not during it.

5 · Mock Interview Script — Card A

How to use: read the examiner lines aloud. [ ] = your actions / cues. ➤ = a model answer to compare against (don't read these to the student). The picture is in words — sketch three quick panels, or just read the panel descriptions aloud.
🟦 PROBLEM CARD A — give to the student
Passage — "Bring Your Own Bag"
These days, many people try to protect the environment in small ways. For example, some people bring their own bags when they go shopping. By doing so, they can reduce the number of plastic bags they use. Some shops also give points to customers who bring their own bags. In this way, stores and customers are working together to help the environment.
Illustration (3 panels)
"One morning, Mika was watching TV at home." ↑ First sentence printed on the card — the student must begin with this.
  • Mika watches the weather news; the screen shows rain in the afternoon. She is not holding an umbrella.
  • "That afternoon" — Mika is at school looking out the window. It is raining hard. She looks worried.
  • "After school" — a friend is sharing an umbrella with Mika. Mika looks happy.
▶ Greeting
Examiner: Hello. May I have your card, please? … Thank you.
Examiner: My name is ______. May I have your name, please? (Student: "My name is …")
Examiner: This is the Grade Pre-2 Plus test. How are you today? (brief chat — not scored)
▶ Reading
Examiner: OK, let's start. Here is your card. First, please read the passage silently for 20 seconds. [wait 20 sec]
Examiner: Now, please read it aloud. [student reads]
▶ No.1 — about the passage
Examiner: Now I'll ask you four questions. No.1 — According to the passage, how do some shops help to reduce the number of plastic bags?
➤ Model: "By giving points to customers who bring their own bags."
▶ No.2 — narration
Examiner: No.2 — Now, please look at the picture and describe the situation. You have 20 seconds to prepare. Your story should begin with the sentence on the card. [wait 20 sec] … Please begin.
➤ Model: "One morning, Mika was watching TV at home. The weather news said it would rain in the afternoon, but she didn't take her umbrella. That afternoon, it was raining hard, and Mika was looking out the window at school. She looked worried because she didn't have an umbrella. After school, her friend was sharing an umbrella with her, so Mika looked happy."
▶ Turn the card over
Examiner: Now, please turn the card over.
▶ No.3 — opinion on the topic
Examiner: No.3 — Do you think more people will bring their own bags when they go shopping in the future?
[If "Yes" →] Why?  ·  [If "No" →] Why not?
➤ Model (Yes): "Yes, I do. Many people want to protect the environment, and bringing a bag is easy. Also, some shops give points, so it is good for shoppers, too."
▶ No.4 — general topic
Examiner: No.4 — Today, many people enjoy outdoor activities such as camping and hiking. Do you like spending time outside?
[If "Yes" →] Please tell me more.  ·  [If "No" →] Why not?
➤ Model (Yes): "Yes, I do. I feel relaxed when I am in nature. For example, I often walk in the park with my family on weekends."
▶ Closing
Examiner: This is the end of the test. May I have the card back, please? … You may go now. Goodbye.

🟩 PROBLEM CARD B
Passage — "Friday at the Library"
Many schools want students to read more books. At one junior high school, the library started a new event. Every Friday, students can borrow two extra books. Teachers also show their favorite books to the students. Since the event began, more students have come to the library, and they enjoy talking about books with their friends.
"One day, Ken was looking for a book in the library." ↑ First sentence printed on the card.
  • Ken can't find the book he wants; he looks troubled.
  • "A few minutes later" — a teacher shows Ken an interesting book.
  • "That night" — Ken is reading the book at home and enjoying it.
No.1: According to the passage, what can students do every Friday?
➤ Model: "They can borrow two extra books."
No.2 (Narration): begin with the sentence on the card.
➤ Model: "One day, Ken was looking for a book in the library, but he couldn't find one he liked. A few minutes later, a teacher showed him an interesting book. That night, Ken was reading the book at home, and he looked very happy."
No.3: Do you think students will read more paper books in the future? [Yes → Why? / No → Why not?]
➤ Model (No): "No, I don't. Many people read on smartphones now because it is convenient, and e-books are often cheaper, so fewer people will buy paper books."
No.4: Today, many people listen to audiobooks. Do you like listening to stories? [Yes → tell me more / No → Why not?]
➤ Model (Yes): "Yes, I do. I can enjoy stories while doing other things. For example, I listen to English stories on the train to school."
🟦 PROBLEM CARD C
Passage — "Cleaning the Park"
In many towns, people work together to keep their parks clean. On weekends, some people join volunteer events and pick up trash in the park. By doing this, they can make the park safer and more beautiful for everyone. Children also learn that taking care of nature is important. Thanks to these events, more people now enjoy spending time outside.
"One Sunday, Kenta saw a poster about a park clean-up event." ↑ First sentence printed on the card.
  • Kenta is reading a poster on the wall about a clean-up event. He looks interested.
  • "The next weekend" — Kenta is picking up trash in the park with many other volunteers.
  • "After the event" — the park looks clean and beautiful, and Kenta feels proud.
No.1: According to the passage, how do some people make the park safer and more beautiful?
➤ Model: "By joining volunteer events and picking up trash in the park."
No.2 (Narration): begin with the sentence on the card.
➤ Model: "One Sunday, Kenta saw a poster about a park clean-up event. He was interested, so he decided to join. The next weekend, Kenta was picking up trash in the park with many other volunteers, and everyone was working hard together. After the event, the park looked clean and beautiful, so Kenta felt very proud."
No.3: Do you think more young people will join volunteer activities in the future? [Yes → Why? / No → Why not?]
➤ Model (Yes): "Yes, I do. Many young people want to help their community, and joining events is a good way to make friends. Schools also tell students about volunteer work, so more of them will take part."
No.4: Today, many people enjoy spending time in parks. Do you like going to parks? [Yes → tell me more / No → Why not?]
➤ Model (Yes): "Yes, I do. Parks are good places to relax and exercise. For example, I often walk my dog or play sports in the park near my house."
🟧 PROBLEM CARD D
Passage — "Walking Every Day"
These days, many people try to stay healthy by walking every day. Some people walk to school or work instead of taking the bus. Others use a smartphone app that counts their steps. By walking more, they can keep their bodies strong and feel less tired. Doctors say that even a short walk every day is good for our health.
"One morning, Yuki decided to start walking to school." ↑ First sentence printed on the card.
  • Yuki is at home putting on her shoes and checking a step-counter app. She decides to walk instead of taking the bus.
  • "Two weeks later" — Yuki is walking to school with her friend and looks full of energy.
  • "One month later" — Yuki is running fast on sports day and feels strong and healthy.
No.1: According to the passage, how do some people keep their bodies strong?
➤ Model: "By walking more every day."
No.2 (Narration): begin with the sentence on the card.
➤ Model: "One morning, Yuki decided to start walking to school instead of taking the bus, and she used an app to count her steps. Two weeks later, Yuki was walking to school with her friend every day, and she felt full of energy. One month later, Yuki was running fast on sports day, and she felt much stronger and healthier than before."
No.3: Do you think more people will use smartphone apps to stay healthy in the future? [Yes → Why? / No → Why not?]
➤ Model (Yes): "Yes, I do. Health apps are easy to use, and they help people check their bodies every day. New apps are also becoming more useful, so more people will use them."
No.4: Today, many people enjoy playing sports. Do you like playing sports? [Yes → tell me more / No → Why not?]
➤ Model (Yes): "Yes, I do. Playing sports is fun and good for my health. For example, I play badminton with my friends after school every week."
🟪 PROBLEM CARD E
Passage — "Talking Online with Family"
Today, many families use the internet to talk with relatives who live far away. With a smartphone or computer, they can see each other's faces and talk for free. By doing this, grandparents can watch their grandchildren grow up, even from another city. Some people also share photos online. Thanks to technology, families can stay close although they live apart.
"One evening, Aya wanted to talk with her grandmother." ↑ First sentence printed on the card.
  • Aya is at home feeling a little lonely. She misses her grandmother, who lives far away, so she picks up a tablet to make a video call.
  • "A few minutes later" — Aya is talking with her grandmother on the screen, and they are both smiling.
  • "That weekend" — Aya shows her grandmother a picture she drew, and her grandmother looks very happy.
No.1: According to the passage, how can grandparents watch their grandchildren grow up from another city?
➤ Model: "By using a smartphone or computer to see each other's faces and talk."
No.2 (Narration): begin with the sentence on the card.
➤ Model: "One evening, Aya wanted to talk with her grandmother, who lived far away. She felt a little lonely, so she decided to make a video call. A few minutes later, Aya was talking with her grandmother on the screen, and they were both smiling. That weekend, Aya showed her grandmother a picture she drew, and her grandmother looked very happy."
No.3: Do you think more people will use the internet to talk with their families in the future? [Yes → Why? / No → Why not?]
➤ Model (Yes): "Yes, I do. The internet makes it easy to talk with people far away, and it is free. Also, more older people are learning to use smartphones, so more families will talk online."
No.4: Today, many people use the internet every day. Do you often use the internet? [Yes → tell me more / No → Why not?]
➤ Model (Yes): "Yes, I do. I use it to study and to talk with my friends. For example, I often watch English videos online to practice listening."

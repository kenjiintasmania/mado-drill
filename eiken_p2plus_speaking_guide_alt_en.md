# EIKEN Grade Pre-2 Plus — Speaking (Second-Stage) Interview
## Guide & Mock-Interview Script for ALTs

> ⚠️ A practical summary (as of June 2026) for running practice interviews. EIKEN does **not** publish per-question point values, so confirm details on the [official page](https://www.eiken.or.jp/eiken/exam/grade_p2plus/solutions.html) and the [Virtual Interview animation](https://www.eiken.or.jp/eiken/exam/virtual/grade_p2plus/).

---

## 1. Quick Overview
- **Format:** one-on-one interview with a single examiner; **entirely in English**
- **Length:** about **6–7 minutes**
- **Level:** between Grade Pre-2 and Grade 2 (upper-secondary). The **narration** task is new compared with Pre-2 and slightly easier than Grade 2.
- **Materials:** a *problem card* with a **~60-word passage** and a **3-panel illustration**

## 2. Format at a Glance
| Task | What the student does | What it checks |
|---|---|---|
| Reading aloud | Read the ~60-word passage | Pronunciation, intonation, fluency |
| **No.1** | Answer a question **about the passage** | Comprehension; answer drawn from the text |
| **No.2 (Narration)** | Describe a **3-panel illustration**; **20 sec prep**, must **begin with the sentence printed on the card** | Past / past-continuous, sequencing, actions & feelings |
| **No.3** | Give own **opinion on the card's topic** | Opinion + reason(s) |
| **No.4** | Give own **opinion on a general/social topic** | Opinion + reason(s) |

> The student turns the card **face-down** before No.3 & No.4.

## 3. Assessment Criteria (5 viewpoints)
**Content** of response · **Pronunciation** · **Vocabulary / Grammar / Usage** · **Amount of information** · **Attitude** (active willingness to communicate — eye contact, audible voice, not giving up).
Results are reported on the **CSE** scale (Speaking out of **625**; passing ≈ **427** — confirm officially). Item-level points are **not** published.

## 4. Coaching Tips for ALTs
- Push for **full sentences** and a **reason** ("…because…").
- For No.2, encourage **time markers** ("One day," "Ten minutes later," "That afternoon,") and **feelings** ("She looked worried.").
- Reward **attitude**: eye contact, a smile, a clear voice — and *tell* the student this counts.
- Teach **stalling phrases** ("Let me see…") and **asking again** ("Could you say that again, please?") so silence never wins.
- Stay encouraging; give corrections **after** the run, not during it.

## 5. MOCK INTERVIEW SCRIPT — Card A
> **How to use:** Read the **examiner lines aloud**. `[ ]` = your actions / cues. `➤` = a **model answer** to compare against (don't read these to the student). The picture is described in words — sketch three quick panels, or just read the panel descriptions aloud.

### 🟦 PROBLEM CARD A — give to the student

**Passage — "Bring Your Own Bag"**
> These days, many people try to protect the environment in small ways. For example, some people bring their own bags when they go shopping. By doing so, they can reduce the number of plastic bags they use. Some shops also give points to customers who bring their own bags. In this way, stores and customers are working together to help the environment.

**Illustration (3 panels). First sentence is printed on the card:**
**"One morning, Mika was watching TV at home."**
- **Panel 1:** Mika watches the weather news; the screen shows **rain in the afternoon**. She is **not** holding an umbrella.
- **Panel 2 — caption *"That afternoon"*:** Mika is at school looking out the window. It is **raining hard**. She looks **worried**.
- **Panel 3 — caption *"After school"*:** A friend is **sharing an umbrella** with Mika. Mika looks **happy**.

### 🎤 Script

**[Greeting]**
- **Examiner:** Hello. May I have your card, please? … Thank you.
- **Examiner:** My name is ______. May I have your name, please?  ➤ *(Student: "My name is …")*
- **Examiner:** This is the Grade Pre-2 Plus test. How are you today?  ➤ *(brief chat — not scored)*

**[Hand over the problem card]**
- **Examiner:** OK, let's start. Here is your card. First, please read the passage **silently for 20 seconds**. `[wait 20 sec]`
- **Examiner:** Now, please read it **aloud**.  `[student reads]`

**[No.1 — about the passage]**
- **Examiner:** Now I'll ask you four questions. **No.1 —** According to the passage, how do some shops help to reduce the number of plastic bags?
  - ➤ *"By giving points to customers who bring their own bags."*

**[No.2 — narration]**
- **Examiner:** **No.2 —** Now, please look at the picture and describe the situation. You have **20 seconds** to prepare. Your story should **begin with the sentence on the card**. `[wait 20 sec]` … Please begin.
  - ➤ *"One morning, Mika was watching TV at home. The weather news said it would rain in the afternoon, but she didn't take her umbrella. That afternoon, it was raining hard, and Mika was looking out the window at school. She looked worried because she didn't have an umbrella. After school, her friend was sharing an umbrella with her, so Mika looked happy."*

**[Turn the card over]**
- **Examiner:** Now, please **turn the card over**.

**[No.3 — opinion on the topic]**
- **Examiner:** **No.3 —** Do you think more people will bring their own bags when they go shopping in the future?
  - `[If "Yes" →]` Why?   `[If "No" →]` Why not?
  - ➤ *(Yes) "Yes, I do. Many people want to protect the environment, and bringing a bag is easy. Also, some shops give points, so it is good for shoppers, too."*

**[No.4 — general topic]**
- **Examiner:** **No.4 —** Today, many people enjoy outdoor activities such as camping and hiking. Do you like spending time outside?
  - `[If "Yes" →]` Please tell me more.   `[If "No" →]` Why not?
  - ➤ *(Yes) "Yes, I do. I feel relaxed when I am in nature. For example, I often walk in the park with my family on weekends."*

**[Closing]**
- **Examiner:** This is the end of the test. May I have the card back, please? … You may go now. Goodbye.

## 7. Spare Cards — for more practice runs (B–E)

### Card B — "Friday at the Library" (reading)
**Passage — "Friday at the Library"**
> Many schools want students to read more books. At one junior high school, the library started a new event. Every Friday, students can borrow two extra books. Teachers also show their favorite books to the students. Since the event began, more students have come to the library, and they enjoy talking about books with their friends.

- **No.1 —** According to the passage, what can students do every Friday?  ➤ *"They can borrow two extra books."*

**Illustration — first sentence: "One day, Ken was looking for a book in the library."**
- **Panel 1:** Ken can't find the book he wants; he looks **troubled**.
- **Panel 2 — *"A few minutes later"*:** A teacher **shows Ken an interesting book**.
- **Panel 3 — *"That night"*:** Ken is **reading the book at home** and enjoying it.
  - ➤ *"One day, Ken was looking for a book in the library, but he couldn't find one he liked. A few minutes later, a teacher showed him an interesting book. That night, Ken was reading the book at home, and he looked very happy."*

- **No.3 —** Do you think students will read more paper books in the future? `[Yes → Why? / No → Why not?]`
  - ➤ *(No) "No, I don't. Many people read on smartphones now because it is convenient, and e-books are often cheaper, so fewer people will buy paper books."*
- **No.4 —** Today, many people listen to audiobooks. Do you like listening to stories? `[Yes → tell me more / No → Why not?]`
  - ➤ *(Yes) "Yes, I do. I can enjoy stories while doing other things. For example, I listen to English stories on the train to school."*

### Card C — "Cleaning the Park" (community / volunteering)
**Passage — "Cleaning the Park"**
> In many towns, people work together to keep their parks clean. On weekends, some people join volunteer events and pick up trash in the park. By doing this, they can make the park safer and more beautiful for everyone. Children also learn that taking care of nature is important. Thanks to these events, more people now enjoy spending time outside.

- **No.1 —** According to the passage, how do some people make the park safer and more beautiful?  ➤ *"By joining volunteer events and picking up trash in the park."*

**Illustration — first sentence: "One Sunday, Kenta saw a poster about a park clean-up event."**
- **Panel 1:** Kenta is reading a poster on the wall about a clean-up event. He looks **interested**.
- **Panel 2 — *"The next weekend"*:** Kenta is **picking up trash** in the park with many other volunteers.
- **Panel 3 — *"After the event"*:** The park looks **clean and beautiful**, and Kenta feels **proud**.
  - ➤ *"One Sunday, Kenta saw a poster about a park clean-up event. He was interested, so he decided to join. The next weekend, Kenta was picking up trash in the park with many other volunteers, and everyone was working hard together. After the event, the park looked clean and beautiful, so Kenta felt very proud."*

- **No.3 —** Do you think more young people will join volunteer activities in the future? `[Yes → Why? / No → Why not?]`
  - ➤ *(Yes) "Yes, I do. Many young people want to help their community, and joining events is a good way to make friends. Schools also tell students about volunteer work, so more of them will take part."*
- **No.4 —** Today, many people enjoy spending time in parks. Do you like going to parks? `[Yes → tell me more / No → Why not?]`
  - ➤ *(Yes) "Yes, I do. Parks are good places to relax and exercise. For example, I often walk my dog or play sports in the park near my house."*

### Card D — "Walking Every Day" (health / exercise)
**Passage — "Walking Every Day"**
> These days, many people try to stay healthy by walking every day. Some people walk to school or work instead of taking the bus. Others use a smartphone app that counts their steps. By walking more, they can keep their bodies strong and feel less tired. Doctors say that even a short walk every day is good for our health.

- **No.1 —** According to the passage, how do some people keep their bodies strong?  ➤ *"By walking more every day."*

**Illustration — first sentence: "One morning, Yuki decided to start walking to school."**
- **Panel 1:** Yuki is at home putting on her shoes and checking a **step-counter app**. She decides to **walk instead of taking the bus**.
- **Panel 2 — *"Two weeks later"*:** Yuki is **walking to school with her friend** and looks **full of energy**.
- **Panel 3 — *"One month later"*:** Yuki is **running fast on sports day** and feels **strong and healthy**.
  - ➤ *"One morning, Yuki decided to start walking to school instead of taking the bus, and she used an app to count her steps. Two weeks later, Yuki was walking to school with her friend every day, and she felt full of energy. One month later, Yuki was running fast on sports day, and she felt much stronger and healthier than before."*

- **No.3 —** Do you think more people will use smartphone apps to stay healthy in the future? `[Yes → Why? / No → Why not?]`
  - ➤ *(Yes) "Yes, I do. Health apps are easy to use, and they help people check their bodies every day. New apps are also becoming more useful, so more people will use them."*
- **No.4 —** Today, many people enjoy playing sports. Do you like playing sports? `[Yes → tell me more / No → Why not?]`
  - ➤ *(Yes) "Yes, I do. Playing sports is fun and good for my health. For example, I play badminton with my friends after school every week."*

### Card E — "Talking Online with Family" (technology / family)
**Passage — "Talking Online with Family"**
> Today, many families use the internet to talk with relatives who live far away. With a smartphone or computer, they can see each other's faces and talk for free. By doing this, grandparents can watch their grandchildren grow up, even from another city. Some people also share photos online. Thanks to technology, families can stay close although they live apart.

- **No.1 —** According to the passage, how can grandparents watch their grandchildren grow up from another city?  ➤ *"By using a smartphone or computer to see each other's faces and talk."*

**Illustration — first sentence: "One evening, Aya wanted to talk with her grandmother."**
- **Panel 1:** Aya is at home feeling a little **lonely**. She misses her grandmother, who lives far away, so she picks up a tablet to **make a video call**.
- **Panel 2 — *"A few minutes later"*:** Aya is **talking with her grandmother on the screen**, and they are both **smiling**.
- **Panel 3 — *"That weekend"*:** Aya **shows her grandmother a picture she drew**, and her grandmother looks **very happy**.
  - ➤ *"One evening, Aya wanted to talk with her grandmother, who lived far away. She felt a little lonely, so she decided to make a video call. A few minutes later, Aya was talking with her grandmother on the screen, and they were both smiling. That weekend, Aya showed her grandmother a picture she drew, and her grandmother looked very happy."*

- **No.3 —** Do you think more people will use the internet to talk with their families in the future? `[Yes → Why? / No → Why not?]`
  - ➤ *(Yes) "Yes, I do. The internet makes it easy to talk with people far away, and it is free. Also, more older people are learning to use smartphones, so more families will talk online."*
- **No.4 —** Today, many people use the internet every day. Do you often use the internet? `[Yes → tell me more / No → Why not?]`
  - ➤ *(Yes) "Yes, I do. I use it to study and to talk with my friends. For example, I often watch English videos online to practice listening."*
